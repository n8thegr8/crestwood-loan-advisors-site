Tips to Help You Avoid Common Scams

Your financial security is important to us. Fraudsters continue to develop new ways to target individuals through phone calls, emails, text messages, mail, and online communications.

Protect Your Personal Information

  • Never provide personal or account information in response to unsolicited requests.
  • Financial institutions will not ask for highly sensitive information (SSN, passwords, PINs) through unsolicited calls or messages.
  • Limit the amount of personal information you share on social media.

Secure Your Accounts and Devices

  • Use strong, unique passwords for financial and online accounts.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication when available.
  • Keep your computer, mobile device, and security software up to date.
  • Log out of financial accounts when using shared computers.

Monitor Your Financial Activity

  • Review account statements and transaction history regularly.
  • Set up account alerts to notify you of large transactions.
  • Report suspicious activity as soon as it is detected.
  • Be alert to investment-related scams.

Use Caution Online

  • Do not click on ads or emails offering free gift cards, prizes, or unexpected refunds.
  • Avoid responding to suspicious emails, texts, or automated calls.
  • Avoid clicking links or downloading attachments from unknown senders.

When in Doubt, Verify

Fraudsters often create urgency or pressure to act quickly. Take your time. If an offer sounds too good to be true, it likely is. Be skeptical, and always verify the authenticity of unsolicited calls, emails, or messages before responding.
